이 글은 Data Commentary의 *두 가지 중심 작업*을 다룹니다 — 데이터 *비교 표현*과 *Highlighting Statement* 작성. 둘 다 *판단력*이 핵심입니다.

## Highlighting Statements — 좋은 판단

Data Commentary의 중심에는 *highlighting statement*가 있습니다. 데이터의 *세부 사항으로 뒷받침되는 요점*입니다. Highlighting은 작가의 *지능을 보일 기회*입니다.

```
□ 데이터의 추세나 규칙성을 찾아낸다
□ 더 중요한 발견과 덜 중요한 것을 구분한다
□ 적절한 강도의 주장을 한다
```

피해야 할 것:

```
□ 단순히 모든 세부 사항을 말로 반복하기
□ 모든 정보를 다 다루려 하기
□ 합리적이거나 옹호 가능한 수준을 넘어선 주장
```

### 학생 사례 — 같은 표, 세 가지 commentary

```
TABLE 10. Decision-Making Patterns of U.S. Parents and Adolescents
                              Total Sample (%)   Girls (%)   Boys (%)

Adolescent Child is Sole Decision-Maker
  Amount of allowance              2              2           3
  Clothes                         28             29          27
  Spending                        50             50          51
  Friends                         53             52          54
  Curfew                           2              2           3
  Television                      42             44          41
  Religion                        23             22          26

Parents Are Sole Decision-Makers
  Amount of allowance             91             91          92
  Clothes                         39             32          45
  Spending                        27             25          29
  Friends                         30             28          31
  Curfew                          88             88          88
  Television                      43             41          45
  Religion                        60             56          64
```

같은 표를 보고 세 학생이 작성한 commentary입니다.

#### Student A

```
Table 10 shows who makes important decisions in key aspects of
adolescents' lives. As can be seen, parents alone are responsible for the
amount of allowance for 91% of girls and 92% of boys. Another category
where parents exert a lot of control is curfew, as revealed by 88% of all
adolescents. Most decisions about religion are also made by parents. In
this category, however, there is a difference between boys and girls. Fifty-
six percent of girls report that their parents decide matters of faith in
contrast to 64% of boys. However, nearly one-fourth of the adolescents
make decisions about religion on their own.
```

#### Student B

```
Table 10 shows the percentage of adolescents and parents who are solely
responsible for important decisions in the lives of adolescents. As can be
seen, decision-making patterns are very similar for both boys and girls
for all types of decisions except one. Specifically, more boys than girls
report parental involvement in clothing decisions. In this category, 45%
of the boys reported sole parental decision making, but only 32% of girls
did so.
```

#### Student C

```
Table 10 shows the decision-making patterns of parents and adolescents
in relation to key aspects of adolescents' lives. As can be seen, overall,
parents are similarly involved in decisions for both boys and girls, but
the level of involvement differs depending on the type of decision. The
percentage of sole parental decision making is highest for the amount of
allowance, the time of curfew, and religion. The lowest percentages were
reported for decisions regarding the child's friends and spending of money.
Television viewing is the one area where similar percentages of children
and their parents make decisions.
```

### 세 commentary 비교

```
Student A — 구체적 항목 나열 (91%, 88%, 56%, 64%, ...). 그러나 큰 그림 약함.
Student B — 큰 그림 잡음 (sex 차이는 한 항목만). 다른 패턴은 무시.
Student C — 두 큰 그림 (성별 유사함 + 결정 유형별 차이). 가장 균형 잡힘.
```

*Student C*가 가장 좋은 highlighting입니다 — 데이터의 *두 가지 큰 패턴*을 인식하고, 세부 사항을 *분류*해서 보입니다.

좋은 highlighting의 공통점:

- *모든 숫자를 말로 옮기지 않음*
- *데이터의 패턴(추세, 그룹화, 대조)*을 식별
- *높은 자리와 낮은 자리*를 구분
- *예외*를 인지

## Comparisons — 비교 표현

데이터 사이의 비교를 학술 영어로 다양하게 표현할 수 있습니다.

### 약한 비교 — 단순 반복

```
약함:
Fifty-six percent of girls report that their parents decide matters of faith
in contrast to 64% of boys.
```

수치를 *그대로 옮기는* 비교는 독자가 *숫자를 못 읽는다*고 가정하는 것처럼 보입니다.

### 강한 비교 — 관계 표현

```
강함:
Fewer girls than boys reported that their parents decide matters of faith.

또는:
More boys than girls reported that their parents decide matters of faith.

또는:
Not as many boys reported that their parents decide matters of faith.
```

*Fewer, more, not as many*가 *비교의 의미*를 명시합니다. 수치는 *표가 보여 줍니다*. 본문은 *해석*에 집중.

### 정량적 비교

*More* / *fewer*만으로는 *얼마나*가 모호합니다. 더 명확한 비교가 필요할 때:

```
모호: More boys than girls reported ...
명확: Eight percent more boys than girls reported ...
```

### 배수 비교

```
TABLE 11. Bicycle Commuters' Perception of Danger While Using Mobile Phones
              Men (n=1000)   Women (n=1000)
Not at all       5.1%             1.8%
Slight          15.8%             7.3%
Moderate        42.6%            46.9%
Quite a bit     24.8%            33.3%
Extreme         11.7%            10.7%
```

이 표에서 *Not at all* 응답이 남성(5.1%)이 여성(1.8%)의 *거의 3배*입니다. 여러 표현이 가능합니다.

```
a. Almost twice as many women as men reported [Quite a bit]...
b. A smaller percentage of women reported [Not at all]...
c. Nearly three times as many men reported [Not at all]...
d. Nearly the same number of men and women [Extreme]...
```

```
e. The percentage of men who thought there was only a slight risk was
   over twice that of women.
f. The percentage of men who thought there was only a slight risk was
   over two times higher than that of women.
g. The percentage of women who thought there was quite a bit of risk
   exceeded that of men.
```

선택 — 작가가 *어느 측면*을 강조하고 싶은지에 따라.

### 비교 표현 도구함

```
배수:
twice as ... as
three times as ... as
half as ... as
over twice that of
N times higher / lower than

차이:
N% more / fewer ... than
N more / fewer
a difference of N
the difference is N%

순서:
the highest / lowest ...
the second highest ...
in descending / ascending order

유사:
similar to
comparable to
roughly equivalent to
in the same range as

차이:
in contrast to
unlike
whereas
while
```

## Organization — 비교 데이터의 구조

데이터 commentary는 보통 *general → specific*. 비교 데이터에서는 결정이 더 복잡해집니다.

### 결정의 축

```
1. 큰 그림부터? 구체적 차이부터?
2. 무엇이 가장 중요한지를 어떻게 정할 것인가?
3. 모든 항목을 다룰 것인가, 일부만?
4. 추세를 한 줄로? 사례별로?
```

Student C의 commentary가 *큰 그림 → 구체적 차이*의 모범 사례입니다.

```
[큰 그림] 부모의 관여가 boys/girls에 *유사*
[구체적 차이] 결정 유형별로 다름
[높은 자리] allowance, curfew, religion
[낮은 자리] friends, spending
[예외] television
```

다섯 단계가 한 단락 안에 짜여 있습니다. 모든 숫자를 다 옮기는 대신 *관찰의 층위*를 만듭니다.

## 박사 학위 사례 — Doctorate Years

```
TABLE 12. Median Years to Doctorate for Selected Doctoral Programs in the US
                 All  LifeSci  PhysSci  SocSci  Eng  Educ  Hum  Other
Median            7.7  7.0    6.7      7.7   6.9  12.3   9.5   9.7
...
US citizens       7.7  6.7    6.2      7.7   6.3  12.8   9.7  10.7
Temp visa         7.7  7.7    7.3      8.2   7.3   9.0   9.3   8.7
```

```
❶ Table 12 shows the median number of years to complete a doctoral
   program for both U.S. and international students (indicated by
   temporary visa status). 
❷ As can be seen, U.S. students in most fields on average complete
   doctoral programs in less time than international students. 
❸ The difference in median years to completion ranges from a relatively
   low 0.4 years in the humanities to a high of one year in the life sciences.
❹ The consistent difference in time to degree is not fully understood at
   present. 
❺ However, one key factor may be the students' relationships with
   their advisors.
❻ Advisors typically expect their advisees to work independently, an
   expectation that might run contrary to the expectations of international
   student advisees, who may expect their advisors to give explicit input
   with regard to how to navigate their way through the doctoral program.
❼ Expectations that are "unmet, unclear, or unarticulated" (Barnes, 2010)
   could influence the length of time it takes to earn a doctorate.
```

분석:

- ❶ — *Indicative location statement* (표가 무엇을 보여 주는지)
- ❷ — *Highlighting* + *headhedge* (*on average, in most fields*)
- ❸ — *정량적 비교* (*0.4 years … one year*)
- ❹ — *Hedge* (*not fully understood at present*)
- ❺–❼ — *Discussion + 해석* (*may, might, could influence*)

7 문장이 *위치 → 부각 → 비교 → 해석*의 전체 사이클을 보입니다. 모든 문장에 *적절한 헤지*가 박혀 있어 *학술적 자신감*과 *지적 겸손함*이 균형을 이룹니다.

## 비교 표현 점검 — Task Eleven

다음 강한 주장들을 *학술적으로 옹호 가능하게* 약화시켜 봅니다.

```
1. Tall people have higher incomes than short people.
   → On average, taller individuals tend to have higher incomes than
     shorter individuals, although the size of this difference varies across
     studies and populations.

2. Economic sanctions are ineffective.
   → Recent research suggests that economic sanctions are often less
     effective than initially expected, particularly in achieving long-term
     policy goals.

3. Alcohol causes brain damage in teenagers.
   → Heavy alcohol consumption during adolescence appears to be
     associated with adverse effects on developing brain structures,
     although the mechanisms remain incompletely understood.

4. Passive smoking causes cancer.
   → Considerable evidence indicates that prolonged exposure to second-
     hand smoke increases the risk of certain cancers.
```

원래 강한 주장이 *적절한 헤지와 한정*을 더해 학술적으로 가능해졌습니다.

## 정리

- **Highlighting**의 핵심은 *판단* — 모든 숫자가 아니라 *패턴, 추세, 예외*
- 좋은 highlighting의 공통점 — 큰 그림 + 구체적 분류 + 예외 인지
- **비교 표현** — *fewer, more, not as many, twice as many, N% more*
- **수치 그대로 옮기지 말 것** — 본문은 *해석*에 집중
- **Organization** — 큰 그림 → 구체적 차이 → 높은 자리 / 낮은 자리 → 예외
- 모든 비교에 *적절한 헤지*

## 한국 학생의 비교 함정

```
1. 모든 수치 나열 → 큰 패턴 / 예외만
2. 비교 표현 단조 ("more than" 만)
   → twice as many, N% higher 다양화
3. 헤지 없이 강한 비교
4. 큰 그림 누락 → highlighting 먼저
```

## 자기 글 점검

```
□ 큰 그림 → 구체 비교 → 예외 순서?
□ 비교 표현 다양?
□ 모든 비교에 적절한 헤지?
□ Highlighting이 *판단*?
```
